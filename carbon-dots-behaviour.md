# Background and Overview

Carbon dots are carbon-based nanomaterial synthesized for many application due to its structure and optical property. The carbon dot material absorbs light in UV specturm and emits it in visible light spectrum. Additionally, having low toxicity and high biocompability to a human body make carbon dots such a useful material in biomedical applications, such as biological sensing and for tracking drug distribution throughout the body (Cui et al., 2021).

In this project, I would like to explore several following things:

- Relationship between **synthesis time and temperature** of carbon dots and **intensity and wavelength of carbon dot emission**
- Relationship between **Synthesis time and temperature** of carbon dots and its **particle size**
- Relationship between **particle size** of carbon dots and its luminescence color
- How to determine **band gap energy of carbon dots**
